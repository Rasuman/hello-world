<h1>Squares and Square Roots</h1>
<p>__________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p>
<h3>Square</h3>
<p>When a number is multiplied by itself, the product is called the <i>square of that number.</i></p>
<p>For Example:</p>
<p>3 x 3 = 3<sup>2</sup> = 9 (We say that 9 is the square of 3.)</p>
<p>16 x 16 = 16<sup>2</sup> = 256 (We say that 256 is the square of 16.)</p>
<p>0.7 x 0.7 = (0.7)<sup>2</sup> = 0.49 (We say that 0.49 is the square if 0.7.)</p>
<h3>Perfect Square</h3
<p>The number 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, and 36 are the squares of counting numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, respectively and are called <i>perfect squares</i> or <i> square numbers</i>.</p>
<p>Example 1.1</p>
<p>Is 900 a perfect square?</p>
<p>Solution:</p>
<p>Resolving into prime factors, we find that 900 = 2 x 2 x 3 x 3 x 5 x 5<br>Since 900 can be grouped into pairs of equal factors, therefore, 900 is a perfect square.<br>Also, 900 = (2 x 3 x 5) x (2 x 3 x 5)= 30 x 30 = (30)<sup>2</sup> 900 is the square of 30.</p>
