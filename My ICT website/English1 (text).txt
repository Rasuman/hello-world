<h1>Poems</h1>
<h3 align="center">Like The Molave</h3>
<p align="center">(Rafael Zulueta Da Costa, 1940)</p><p>_______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p>
<p align="center">Not yet, Rizal, not yet. Sleep not in peace;<br>There are a thousand waters to be spanned;<br>There are a thousand mountains to be crossed;<br>There are a Thousand crosses to be borne.</p>
<p align="center">Our Shoulders are not strong; our sinews are grown flaccid<br>with dependence smug with ease<br>under another's wing, Rest not in peace;<br>Not yet, Rizal, not yet.</p>
<p align="center">The land has need of young blood<br>And what younger than your own,<br>Forever spilled the great name of freedom,<br>Forever oblate the altar of the free?<br>Not you alone, Rizal.</p>
<p align="center">O souls and spirits of the martyed brave, arise!<br>Arise and scour the land!<br>Shed once again your willing blood!<br>Infuse the vibrant red into our thin amenic veins;</p>
<p align="center">Until we pick up your Promethean tools and, strong,<br>Out of the depthless matrix of your faith<br>In us, and on the silent cliffs of freedom,<br>We carve for all time your marmoreal dream!</p>
<p align="center">Until our people,seeing and become<br>Like the Molave, firm, resilent, staunch<br>Rising on the hillside, unafraid<br>Strong on its own fiber, yes, like the molave!</p>
<p align="center">Not yet, Rizal, Not yet.<br>The glory hour will come<br>Out of the silent dreaming<br>from the seven thousand fold silence</p>
<p align="center">We shall emerge, saying WE ARE FILIPINOS!<br>and no longer be ashamed<br>Sleep not in peace<br>the dream is not yet fully carved</p>
<p align="center">hard the wood but harder the woods<br>yet the molave will stand<br>yet the molave monument will rise<br>and gods walk on brown legs.</p>

<tr><td><h3 align="center">Lyric 17</h3>
<p align="center">(Jose Garcia Villa)</p>
<p align="center">First, a poem must be magical,<br>Then musical as a seagull.<br>It must be a brightness moving<br>And hold secret a bird's flowering<br>It must be slender as a bell,<br>And it must hold fire as well.<br>
It must have the wisdom of bows<br>And it must kneel like a rose<br>It must be able to hide<br>What it seeks, like a bride.<br>And over all I would like ti hover<br>God, smiling from the poem's cover.</p>
<h3 align="center">To a lovely woman</h3>
<p align="center">(Angela Manalang-Gloria, 1935)</p>
<p align="center">Shall I compare you to a rainbowed shower<br>Drawing to earth the very arc of dream,<br>Or shall I say you are an orchid flower<br>That fevers men beside a jungle stream?<br>Because you stir the sleeping blood and brain<br>
To rise and follow beauty till beauty, scourning<br>Desire's fleet runners, vanishes again?<br>No ,you are more than spectrum, than the find<br>Of orchid hunters, than Ariel dawn on wings<br>And I, who know you are the undefined<br>
Reality of all unreal things<br>Now wisely set your breathlessness apart<br>As the unanswered challenge to the dreamer's art.</p>
